Import your Magic: The Gathering collection

SpellControl imports a collection from any popular tracking tool. Paste your export or drop the file — the format is auto-detected, so you never pick one from a menu. Importing is free and needs no account: your cards stay on your device unless you sign in to sync them across devices.

Guides by source

  • ManaBox

    CSV or TSV export. The Scryfall ID column pins every card to its exact printing, and your ManaBox binder names come along.

  • Moxfield

    Collection CSV export. Edition and collector number resolve exact printings; condition, language, foil, and purchase price are kept.

  • Archidekt

    Collection CSV export with condition codes, finish, language, and price.

  • Deckbox

    Inventory CSV export. Wishlist-only rows are skipped, owned copies come through.

  • MTG Arena & text lists

    Arena deck exports, EDHREC/Moxfield deck lists, or a plain list of card names — one card per line.

  • Any CSV or spreadsheet

    TCGplayer, Cardsphere, or your own spreadsheet — any file with a Name column imports.

What every import keeps

SpellControl reads whatever your file provides:

  • Exact printing — via a Scryfall ID when the export has one, otherwise name + set + collector number.
  • Quantity per card.
  • Finish — nonfoil, foil, or etched.
  • Condition and language per copy, normalized across tools (NM, Near Mint, and Mint all land in the same bucket).
  • Purchase price, kept on the card.
  • Binder or folder name from the source tool, kept as each card's source category.

After import, cards file themselves into rule-based binders — sorted by color, type, set, price, tags, or any rules you define.
